# UCSD Student Knowledge Base

## RESIDENTIAL COLLEGES

UCSD has 8 residential colleges, each with its own GE requirements and writing sequence.

### Revelle College (Founded 1964)
- Philosophy: Renaissance education — broad foundation across all disciplines
- Writing: Humanities (HUM) — 5-quarter interdisciplinary sequence
- Location: Southwest campus, facing Pacific Ocean
- GE: Most rigorous (16-20 courses); heavy math/science even for non-STEM majors

### John Muir College (Founded 1967)
- Philosophy: Self-sufficiency and individual choice
- Writing: MCWP — 2 courses (1 for transfers)
- Location: Southwest campus, near Geisel Library
- GE: Most flexible/minimal (11-14 courses); most popular college choice

### Thurgood Marshall College (Founded 1970)
- Philosophy: Scholarship and social responsibility
- Writing: Dimensions of Culture (DOC) — 3 courses (6 units each)
- Location: West campus, near RIMAC Arena
- GE: Social justice focus; newer luxury housing at Ridge Walk LLN

### Earl Warren College
- Philosophy: Ethics and Society
- Writing: Warren Writing (2 courses) + Ethics (2 courses)
- Location: Northeast campus, near I-5
- GE: Engineering-friendly; two "Programs of Concentration" outside major; fewest GE units tied with Muir

### Eleanor Roosevelt College (ERC, Founded 1988)
- Philosophy: International understanding and global perspective
- Writing: Making of the Modern World (MMW) — 5-course interdisciplinary sequence
- Location: Northwest campus, facing Pacific Ocean
- GE: Foreign language requirement; encourages study abroad; 24 writing-related units

### Sixth College (Founded 2001)
- Philosophy: Culture, Art, and Technology connections
- Writing: CAT — 3-quarter core sequence
- Location: West campus, newer residence halls
- GE: 11 lower-division breadth courses; tech fluency and art-making; 24 writing-related units

### Seventh College (Founded 2020)
- Philosophy: "A Changing Planet" — climate, sustainability, racial justice
- Writing: Synthesis — 3-course project-based sequence
- Location: Northwest campus, near athletic facilities
- GE: Apartment-style housing; interdisciplinary global challenges

### Eighth College (Founded 2021, first cohort Fall 2023)
- Philosophy: "Engagement & Community" — anti-racism, wellness, social justice
- Writing: CCE — 4-course writing-intensive series
- Location: Southwest campus, Theatre District LLN (newest dorms)
- GE: ~14 courses; interdisciplinary research emphasis

## TRANSPORTATION

### MTS Bus Routes
- Route 30: UTC → La Jolla → Pacific Beach → Old Town → Downtown
- Route 41: VA Hospital → UTC → Clairemont → Fashion Valley
- Routes 201/202 (SuperLoop): UTC area circulation
- Route 237: UCSD → Rancho Bernardo Transit Station
- Route 974: UCSD COASTER Connector → Sorrento Valley Station

### Blue Line Trolley
- Light rail directly to campus at Central Campus Station (near Epstein Family Amphitheater)
- Connects to Downtown San Diego

### Triton U-Pass
- Included with student fees ($75/quarter Transportation Fee)
- Unlimited MTS bus, trolley, and NCTD rides
- Activate via PRONTO account with @ucsd.edu email
- Website: u-pass.ucsd.edu

### Campus Shuttles (Free)
- Outside Loop: Campus perimeter circulation
- SIO Shuttle: Main campus ↔ Scripps Institution of Oceanography (every 15 min weekdays)
- Weekend Grocery Shuttle: Campus → Clairemont/Convoy shopping

### Parking
- S Permits (Commuter): Daily purchase, upper-division only on central campus
- D Permits: Shuttle lot parking
- SuperSaver: Walk-in lots, cheapest option
- Lower-division students limited to D and SuperSaver

## KEY ACADEMIC POLICIES

### Grading
- Scale: A+/A = 4.0, A- = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B- = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C- = 1.7, D = 1.0, F = 0.0
- GPA = total grade points / total letter-graded units

### P/NP (Pass/Not Pass)
- P = C- or better; earns units, no GPA impact
- Deadline: First 6 weeks of the course
- Limit: No more than 25% of total UCSD units may be P/NP

### Academic Standing
- Academic Notice: Term or cumulative GPA below 2.0
- Subject to Disqualification: Term GPA below 1.5, OR two successive quarters on notice without reaching 2.0
- Minimum Progress: 36 units in any 3 consecutive enrollment quarters
- Minimum 2.0 cumulative GPA required for bachelor's degree

### Academic Integrity
- Website: academicintegrity.ucsd.edu
- Violators must complete mandatory Ethics Lab
- Sanctions: warning → probation → suspension → dismissal

### Change of Major / Selective Majors
- General: students.ucsd.edu/academics/advising/majors-minors/declare-or-change-major.html
- Selective (Capped) Majors (CSE, Data Science, ECE, etc.): Competitive application, August 1 - September 1 annually
- Must complete screening courses with minimum 2.0 UC GPA
- Selection based on: screening course GPA, CA residency, first-gen status, Pell eligibility
- No appeals for non-selection
